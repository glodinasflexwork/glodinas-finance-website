Bank Reconciliation in Moneybird

Ensuring your bank statements match your Moneybird records

What is Bank Reconciliation?

Bank reconciliation is the process of comparing your company's bank statements with your internal financial records in Moneybird. This ensures that both sets of records are accurate and in agreement.

Why is it Important?

Reconciliation helps identify discrepancies, detect errors or fraud, and ensures that your financial statements are reliable. It's a crucial step for accurate financial reporting.

Steps in Moneybird

1

Import Bank Statements

Moneybird allows you to import bank statements directly or connect your bank account for automatic synchronization.

2

Match Transactions

Moneybird intelligently suggests matches between bank transactions and your recorded invoices or expenses. Review and confirm these matches.

3

Categorize Unmatched Items

For transactions that don't automatically match, categorize them manually (e.g., new expenses, income, or transfers).

4

Review and Finalize

Once all transactions are matched or categorized, review the reconciliation summary and finalize the process. Any remaining differences should be investigated.
